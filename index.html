Shigeru Miyamoto

November 16, 1952 - XXX

Shigeru Miyamoto (Japanese: 宮本 茂, Hepburn: Miyamoto Shigeru, pronounced [mijamoto ɕiɡeɾɯ]; born November 16, 1952) is a Japanese video game designer, producer and game director at Nintendo, where he serves as one of its representative directors.

Achievements

He first helped the company develop a game with the 1980 release 'Radar Scope'. The game achieved moderate success in Japan.
On April 22, 1981 'Donkey Kong' was the first game from Nintendo that was released in America. The success of Donkey Kong led to the work of its sequels ‘Donkey Kong Jr.’ (1982) and ‘Donkey Kong 3’ (1983).
In 1983 he created the iconic game 'Mario Bros' based on the character played in 'Donkey Kong', he remodeled it to represent Mario and added Luigi alongside it. It became a great success, releasing in many platforms.
After the relrease of 'Mario Bros', he created a sequel to it called 'Super Mario Bros' which released in 1985 and became a succsess as well.
On February 21, 1986 Nintendo released 'The Legend of Zelda' created by Miyamoto and Takashi Tezuka becoming a best-seller.
The first video game developer to in the 'Hall of Fame' from the Academy of Interative Arts and Sciences in 1997.
Developed one of Nintendo's best video game consoles the 'Wii' which released in November 19, 2006.
French Minister of Culture, Renaud Donnedieu de Vabres, honoured him as a Chevalier (knight) of the French Ordre des Arts et des Lettres.
